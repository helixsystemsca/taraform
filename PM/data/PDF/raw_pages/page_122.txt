2.1.6.1 Initiate Project or Phase
The Initiate Project or Phase process officially authorizes the start of a project and grants the project
manager the authority to allocate organizational resources to project activities by creating a project
charter or similar document. Authorization—typically, but not always, via a charter—establishes a
direct link between the project, the business case, and the organization’s strategic goals, creating an
official record of the project and demonstrating the organization’s commitment to the project. This
process is usually carried out once or at certain points during the life cycle of a project, depending on
the project development approach (see Figure 2-3).
