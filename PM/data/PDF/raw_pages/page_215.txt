3.5 Tailoring the Performance Domains
The work associated with each performance domain can also be tailored, based on the uniqueness of
the project. As shown in Figure 3-4, the principles for project management provide guidance for the
behavior of project practitioners as they tailor the performance domains to meet the unique needs of
the project context and environment. Tailoring consideration examples for each project management
performance domain are specifically covered in Section 2 on performance domains.
3.6 Diagnostics
Periodic reviews, such as retrospectives or lessons learned, help assess the effectiveness of
current approaches and identify areas for improvement through tailoring. Teams that do not use
retrospectives can rely on issues, threats, quality metrics, and stakeholder feedback to determine if
further tailoring is needed. Table 3-1 provides common situations and tailoring suggestions.
