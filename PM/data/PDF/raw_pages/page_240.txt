Section 4 – Inputs and Outputs
⚫ Risk categories. Risk categories provide a means for grouping individual project risks.
A common way to structure risk categories is with a risk breakdown structure (RBS), which
is a hierarchical representation of potential sources of risk (see an example in Figure 4-5).
An RBS helps the project team consider the full range of sources from which individual
project risks may arise. This view can be useful when identifying risks or when categorizing
identified risks. The organization may have a generic RBS to be used for all projects,
