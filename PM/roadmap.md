CAPM Roadmap — From Zero to Application Ready

PURPOSE

Provide a clear, step-by-step path from no formal PM experience to being ready to apply for CAPM.

---

🧭 PHASE 1 — Foundations (Weeks 1–3)

Goal:

Understand how projects work and build decision-thinking habits.

Checklist:

- [ ] Understand project vs operations
- [ ] Learn project lifecycle (start → finish)
- [ ] Learn basic roles (PM, stakeholder, team)
- [ ] Complete 20–30 scenarios (Fundamentals)
- [ ] Achieve 70%+ accuracy in fundamentals

---

Parallel Track — Experience (START NOW)

- [ ] Identify real tasks at work that can be treated as “projects”
- [ ] Start logging projects in your operations app
- [ ] Record:
  - what was planned
  - what went wrong
  - what you did

---

🧱 PHASE 2 — Core PM Knowledge (Weeks 3–6)

Goal:

Learn structured planning and control

Checklist:

- [ ] Scope (what is included)
- [ ] Schedule (timing and sequencing)
- [ ] Risk (what could go wrong)
- [ ] Stakeholders (who is impacted)
- [ ] Complete 50–80 scenarios

Targets:

- 75%+ accuracy across domains
- Ability to explain WHY an answer is correct

---

Parallel Track — Experience Expansion

- [ ] Coordinate at least 1 real project
- [ ] Track tasks, timelines, and issues
- [ ] Log 3–5 experience entries

---

🧠 PHASE 3 — Applied Thinking (Weeks 6–10)

Goal:

Think like a project manager

Checklist:

- [ ] Diagnose problems before acting
- [ ] Handle ambiguous scenarios
- [ ] Complete multi-step scenarios
- [ ] Build 3–5 full project logs

Target:

- 80%+ accuracy
- Strong explanations, not guesses

---

📊 PHASE 4 — CAPM Readiness (Weeks 10–12)

Goal:

Meet requirements and prepare application

Requirements:

- [ ] 23 hours of project management education
- [ ] Basic understanding of all domains
- [ ] Practice exam readiness

---

Resume & Positioning

Checklist:

- [ ] Convert experience logs into:
  - resume bullets
  - project summaries
- [ ] Build 3 strong project examples
- [ ] Prepare interview answers (STAR format)

---

🎯 FINAL STATE

You are ready when:

- You understand PM structure
- You have real tracked experience
- You can explain your decisions clearly
- You meet CAPM education requirement

---

RULE

Do not move forward without understanding WHY decisions are correct.
